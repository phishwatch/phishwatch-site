Responsible Disclosure

Security contact and vulnerability reporting policy

Short version: If you find a security vulnerability in PhishWatch, email security@phishwatch.io. We will acknowledge within 48 hours and send a status update within 7 days. We ask that you not publish details publicly until we have issued a fix.

1. Security contact

Security email
security@phishwatch.io
Monitored by the PhishWatch security team. Response within 48 hours on business days.

For general support or non-security questions, use the same address. PGP encryption is not currently available — if you need to transmit sensitive proof-of-concept material, request a secure channel in your initial email and we will arrange one.

2. Scope

The following are in scope for responsible disclosure:

AssetIn scope
Chrome extensionAll versions distributed via the Chrome Web Store
Backend APIapi.phishwatch.io — all endpoints
Websitephishwatch.io — all pages including MSP registration
MSP dashboardphishwatch.io/msp/ — registration and tenant dashboard

Out of scope: third-party services we depend on (Render, Google Chrome, RDAP registries), volumetric denial-of-service attacks, findings that require physical access to a user's device, and social engineering attacks against PhishWatch staff.

3. What to report

We are interested in any finding that could compromise user security or privacy. Examples include:

  • Authentication bypass or privilege escalation on any endpoint
  • Cross-site scripting (XSS) or injection vulnerabilities on phishwatch.io
  • Tenant isolation failures — one MSP accessing another tenant's data
  • Extension content script injection or privilege escalation
  • Bypass of the phishing detection logic that could be weaponised at scale
  • API key exposure or insecure credential handling
  • SSRF, path traversal, or unsafe deserialization in the backend
  • Information disclosure from error responses or logs

You do not need to produce a working exploit to report a finding. A clear description of the vulnerability, the affected component, and the potential impact is sufficient.

4. Response commitments

MilestoneTarget
AcknowledgementWithin 48 hours of receipt (business days)
Initial assessmentWithin 7 days — severity classification and fix timeline
Fix or mitigationWithin 30 days for Critical/High findings; 90 days for Medium/Low
Disclosure coordinationWe will notify you before publishing any advisory and agree a disclosure date with you

If we are unable to meet a committed deadline, we will contact you to explain why and agree a revised timeline.

5. What we ask

  • Do not publish vulnerability details publicly until we have issued a fix and coordinated disclosure with you
  • Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users or tenants
  • Do not perform denial-of-service testing against production infrastructure
  • Make a good-faith effort to avoid privacy violations and service disruption during testing
  • Report the finding to us before disclosing to any third party

We commit to the same in return: we will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith and follow these guidelines.

6. Recognition

We will acknowledge security researchers who report valid vulnerabilities in our release notes, unless you prefer to remain anonymous. We do not currently offer a bug bounty programme, but we will recognise every valid report by name (or handle) in the relevant release notes entry.

To be recognised, include your preferred name or handle in your initial report.

7. Contact for other security matters

If you are an MSP or enterprise customer with a security question about a PhishWatch deployment — key rotation, data processing agreements, audit log access, or incident response — contact security@phishwatch.io. We will respond within one business day.
