1. What this agreement covers

PhishWatch grants the Partner a free, non-exclusive, non-transferable right to deploy and evaluate the PhishWatch browser security extension for a period of 30 days (the "Pilot"). The Pilot covers deployment to one client company chosen by the Partner.

This agreement begins on the date both parties sign it and ends automatically after 30 days unless extended in writing by both parties.

2. What PhishWatch commits to

  • Provide access to the PhishWatch extension and MSP dashboard at no charge
  • Review and respond to any false positive report within one business day
  • Provide email support throughout the Pilot period
  • Deliver a written summary report within 5 business days of the Pilot ending
  • Process all data in accordance with the GDPR Data Processing Agreement (available at phishwatch.io/dpa)

3. What the Partner commits to

  • Deploy PhishWatch to at least one client company during the Pilot period
  • Keep telemetry enabled so the dashboard has data to report on
  • Provide honest feedback on what works and what does not
  • Not use PhishWatch for any purpose other than evaluation during the Pilot
  • Inform their client that PhishWatch will be deployed and that anonymised telemetry data will be processed in Frankfurt, Germany

4. Data processing

PhishWatch processes only: destination domain names, signal identifiers, risk band classifications, and anonymous session UUIDs. No page content, passwords, clipboard text, or personal identifiers are ever transmitted or stored.

All data is processed and stored in Frankfurt, Germany. Retention is 90 days, after which data is automatically deleted. The full GDPR Art. 28 Data Processing Agreement is available at phishwatch.io/dpa and is incorporated by reference.

5. No charge — no warranty

The Pilot is provided entirely free of charge. PhishWatch is provided "as is" during the Pilot. No uptime guarantee, no SLA, and no warranty of any kind is given. As the Pilot is provided at no charge, PhishWatch's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed €0.

Neither party shall be liable for indirect, consequential, or incidental damages arising from the use or inability to use PhishWatch during the Pilot.

6. Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep the other's confidential information private. This includes: the Partner's client details and deployment configuration, and PhishWatch's technical architecture and commercial terms. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for two years.

7. Termination

Either party may end this Agreement at any time with 24 hours written notice to the other party. On termination, the Partner removes PhishWatch from all deployed devices via the same Chrome policy used to install it. PhishWatch will delete all Partner telemetry data within 72 hours of a written deletion request to security@phishwatch.io.

8. What happens after the Pilot

This Agreement does not create any obligation for either party to enter a commercial relationship after the Pilot. If the Partner wishes to continue using PhishWatch, a separate commercial agreement will be required.

9. Governing law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Any disputes shall be resolved by the courts of England and Wales, except that either party may seek urgent injunctive relief in any jurisdiction.

10. Entire agreement

This Agreement, together with the GDPR Data Processing Agreement at phishwatch.io/dpa, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the Pilot. It supersedes all prior discussions and representations. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
